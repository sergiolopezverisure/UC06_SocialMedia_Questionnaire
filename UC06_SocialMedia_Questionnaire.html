Why is UC06 priority #1?

UC06 covers two fundamentally different data flows that require separate consent models, separate controls, and separate gap analyses. Running discovery without splitting them leads to incomplete findings in both.

🔵 Flow A riskPixels firing before consent; generic "Advertising" category with no social distinction.
🟢 Flow B riskCRM list uploads to Meta without dedicated social targeting consent.
Common to bothNo audit trail, no central consent validation, no revocation propagation to platforms.
Highest-risk areaManual CSV uploads by agencies (B) and unblocked pixels (A).

1. Business context

Defines the rest of the discovery

What is social media used for?

  • Custom audiences, retargeting, lookalikes
  • Conversion tracking

Which platforms are active?

  • Meta, Google, TikTok, LinkedIn, others?

Who manages campaigns and audiences?

  • Internal team or external agency?
  • Which tools does the agency use?

⚠️ CRITICAL — What is the origin of data used in social?

  • A · Pixel / tracking (web or app)
  • B · Internal tools (CRM / CDP)
  • A + B · Both
Why this question defines everything: The answer determines which flows to run. Most countries will answer "both" — but confirming this early avoids wasted discovery time and ensures no flow is skipped.
🔵
Flow A  ·  Pixel / Tracking-based social

Covers pixels, SDKs, tag managers and any social activation originating from web or app tracking. Some of the questions can be directly seen in the webpage (as example, configuration of CMP). Important to unsderstand that customers may accept cookies before login, and this configuration remains for all the navigation.

2A

Tracking setup

What tracking technologies are deployed?

  • Pixels (Meta, Google Ads, TikTok…)
  • Mobile SDKs
  • Tag managers (GTM, Tealium…)

On which channels?

  • Web, app, or both?
3A

Consent category and management in the banner

Is there a specific category for social media / advertising?

  • Or bundled under a generic "Advertising" category?
  • Separated from Analytics?
  • Separated from general Marketing?

Is the pixel actually blocked until the user consents?

  • Does any pixel fire before acceptance?
  • What happens on rejection — fully blocked or partial data sent?
Target model requirement: Social media / advertising must be a distinct consent category, blocked before acceptance, separate from Analytics and own-marketing categories.
4A

Data collected and identifiers

What data does the pixel collect?

  • Page views, events (conversions), user behaviour
  • Any form data or PII fields?

What identifiers are used?

  • Cookies, device IDs
  • Hashed email/phone?
5A

Purposes of tracking

What is tracking used for?

  • Retargeting
  • Conversion measurement
  • Audience building (lookalikes, custom audiences)

Are these purposes separated in the consent?

  • Or all covered by a single "Advertising" toggle?
  • Can a user accept measurement but reject retargeting?
6A

Cookie consent expiry and re-consent

How long does cookie consent last?

  • Defined expiry period? (6m, 1y…)
  • Stored in the CMP with an expiry date?

How is re-consent handled?

  • Banner re-shown on expiry?
  • What happens if the user does not interact?
Note: Cookie consent expiry (CMP, Flow A) follows a completely different lifecycle from CRM consent expiry (Flow B) — do not treat them as equivalent.
7A

Revocation (tracking)

If a user withdraws or changes cookie consent:

  • Is the pixel immediately deactivated?
  • Does tracking stop being sent to the platform?

Flow B  ·  CRM / First-party data

Covers customer or lead data from internal systems (CRM, CDP) uploaded or synced to social platforms.

2B

Internal data origin

Which internal systems generate the data used in social?

  • CRM, CDP, lead management system

What type of data is used?

  • Email, phone, customer ID
  • Behavioural or transactional data from CRM?
3B

Consent capture

CRITICAL

Where is consent captured?

  • Web forms, preference centre
  • Call centre script, paper / partner form
  • Digital contract / onboarding

Does dedicated consent exist for social media targeting?

  • Specific consent for social targeting?
  • Specific consent for data sharing with platforms?
  • Or is general marketing consent reused?

Is consent stored in the tools? Are the tools connected at consent level?

  • Is consent recorded and retrievable in any internal system (CRM, CDP, marketing tool)?
  • Are those tools in sync — does a consent change in one propagate to the others?
  • Or does each tool manage consent independently with no shared layer?
This is where OneTrust plays a central role: The preference centre and consent capture journeys are the primary touchpoints OneTrust must govern for Flow B.
Target model requirement: Consent for CRM-based social targeting must be captured explicitly and separately from general marketing consent — it is a different purpose and a different data flow.
4B

Identifiers used in social

Which identifiers are used for platform matching?

  • Email, phone, customer ID
  • Multi-key matching?

Are identifiers hashed before upload?

  • SHA-256 or other?
  • Where? CRM, CDP, ad connector, or platform-side?
5B

Audience creation

How are audiences created?

  • Automated integration (ad connector, CDP sync)
  • Manual CSV / file export
  • API (CAPI, Customer Match…)

Are there manual uploads?

  • Who does them — internal team or agency?
  • How are they controlled?
  • Is there a consent check and an audit trail?
Highest-risk area: Manual uploads by agencies with no prior consent check or audit trail are the most common and most critical gap in Flow B.
6B

Consent validation before activation

Typical technical gap

Is consent validated before creating audiences or uploading?

  • Automatic or manual check?
  • CRM query, CDP flag, or no validation?

Where does validation happen?

  • CRM, CDP, marketing tool?
  • Or does no formal validation exist today?
Target model requirement: The system must automatically block any upload or audience activation without valid social media consent — manual controls are not sufficient.
7B

Consent expiry (Verisure tools)

Is there a defined expiry for social media consent in the Verisure tools?

  • Defined validity period? (6m, 1y, 2y…)
  • Expiry date stored alongside the consent record?

Is validity checked before each activation?

  • Do systems check expiry before each upload or sync?
  • Or only at the moment consent was originally captured?

Is there a re-consent process before expiry?

  • Which channel is used for renewal?
  • What happens if the user does not respond?
Note: Verisure tools consent expiry (Flow B) and cookie consent expiry (Flow A) are completely separate — a user may have valid cookie consent but an expired Verisure tools consent. Both must be checked independently.
Silent gap: Audiences loaded from already-expired Verisure tools consents are almost never excluded automatically — very common finding.
8B

Revocation and platform sync

If a customer withdraws CRM-based consent:

  • Are they removed from active platform audiences?
  • How quickly is it propagated?

Are new uploads and audience refresh stopped?

  • Automated syncs, ad connectors, CSV uploads?
  • Automatic or manual?

Is there synchronisation with the platforms?

  • Is the platform notified to suppress or delete the record?
  • Covered by the platform DPA / terms?
Target model requirement: Withdrawal must trigger immediate stop of new uploads, blocking of refresh, removal from existing audiences, and platform notification — without undue delay.

Cross-cutting blocks  ·  Apply to both Flow A and Flow B

Traceability, systems architecture, and gap checklist.

9

Traceability and logging

Are audiences and uploads logged? (Flow B)

  • Date, data type/volume, platform, who uploaded?
  • Both automated and manual uploads?

Is consent evidence linked to each activation?

  • Timestamp, purpose, channel, wording version?
  • Does "social media" appear explicitly as the purpose?

Can data shared with platforms be audited?

  • Transfer log accessible to Privacy / Legal?
  • Chain from consent to activated audience traceable?
Target model requirement: Every data transfer (Flow B) and tracking activation (Flow A) must be fully logged: platform, dataset, timestamp, country, volume, and linked consent evidence.
10

Systems and architecture

Which systems are involved in the social media flow?

  • CRM, CDP, marketing automation (Flow B)
  • CMP, tag manager, ad connectors (Flow A + B)

Where is the consent source of truth per flow?

  • Flow A → CMP
  • Flow B → CRM / CDP
  • Are they the same system or separate?
Target model requirement: A central (or consistently governed) consent validation layer must exist — campaign tools, ad connectors and tracking systems must consume validated eligibility status, not manage their own logic.
11

Risks and gaps

Red flags checklist

Document any confirmed gap in the country gap log.

🔵 Pixel / Tracking gaps

  • Pixel fires before user accepts in the banner?
  • No specific social / advertising category in CMP?
  • All tracking purposes bundled under one toggle?

🟢 CRM / First-party gaps

  • General marketing consent reused for social targeting?
  • Manual uploads with no consent check or audit trail?
  • No pre-upload consent validation in any system?

🔁 Revocation / expiry gaps

  • Withdrawal not propagated to platforms?
  • Expired CRM consents still active in audiences?

🧾 Traceability gaps

  • No audience inventory or upload log?
  • No transfer log accessible to Privacy / Legal?
